# Garden Guide Generator — Business Case

## Hypothesis

For any zip code, a **printable, scannable** guide to native and adapted plants—plus responsible invasive warnings and buy links—can convert location intent into affiliate and premium PDF revenue, especially for gardeners who want paper in hand.

---

## Why This Matters to Me

Location-correct planting reduces waste and supports native species; pairing that with a pleasant printable artifact matches how many people actually use garden advice (kitchen table, not just browser tabs).

---

## Who It's For

Home gardeners who want **local** guidance (not generic national articles), value printable references, and benefit from clear layout—including ADHD-friendly, scannable formatting as a product differentiator.

---

## What It Does

- Generate a PDF guide for a location (zip) with native plants, adapted varieties, invasive cautions, and where to buy
- Freemium / affiliate path with optional premium or print-on-demand upsides
- SEO-friendly long-tail capture (“seeds for [place]”)

Relationship to other work: **Seed Finder** is complementary (discovery vs. printable artifact); **Best Day Ever** informs printable layout craft.

---

## Market

| Segment                  | Size        | Basis                                                            |
| ------------------------ | ----------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Total market (earn pool) | $8M–$25M    | Affiliate + content-style revenue, not total consumer seed spend |
| Reachable segment        | $3M–$12M    | One strong site’s realistic ceiling with SEO                     |
| Year 1 target            | $25K–$75K   | Freemium + affiliate; early PDF sales                            |
| Year 3 target            | $250K–$750K | SEO + optional print-on-demand depth                             |

---

## Existing Options

| Option                           | Strength                       | Limitation                                                    |
| -------------------------------- | ------------------------------ | ------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Seed company sites               | Great catalogs, trusted brands | Not zip-native; overwhelming; weak printable “guide” shape    |
| Garden apps (Gardenize, Planter) | Digital planning               | Not printable-first; not accessibility-optimized for scanning |
| Extension / USDA resources       | Authoritative, local           | Bad UX; not seed-merchant-integrated; not “take to the store” |
| General gardening content sites  | Traffic                        | Not location+printable+seed in one workflow                   |

**Gap:** Location + printable + affiliate/buy path in a single product experience.

---

## Biggest Unknown

SEO velocity and defensibility: niche PDF pages can work, but they can also be copied; data quality and template quality must compound faster than copycats.

---

## Validation Plan

|                | Detail                                                                                                      |
| -------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Method         | Ship free PDFs for a limited set of zips; measure search ranking, download completion, and affiliate clicks |
| Traffic        | Organic long-tail; optional paid on high-intent local queries                                               |
| Budget         | Content + data sourcing + hosting; scale with margin                                                        |
| Success        | Clicks to merchants and/or premium PDF purchases at viable conversion                                       |
| Decision point | If pages don’t rank or don’t convert, narrow geography or improve template before broadening                |

**Status (hub):** Abandoned.

## Recommendation

Market research said **GO** on an MVP built around free PDFs + affiliates. The concept is still coherent; abandonment likely reflects bandwidth vs. invalidity. If you return, start with a **tight region** and measure rank and click-through before automating all zip codes.
