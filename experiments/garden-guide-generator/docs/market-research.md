# Garden Guide Generator - Market Research Report

## Executive Summary

**Garden Guide Generator** is a location-based printable seed guide tool that generates accessible, scannable PDF guides for any zip code showing native plants, adapted varieties, invasive warnings, and where to buy them. The product combines location-based discovery with digital-to-physical transformation and ADHD-friendly formatting to make gardening more accessible.

**Key Findings:**
- **TAM**: $1.20B - $1.52B (US garden seeds market, 2025-2030)
- **SAM**: $245M - $309M (online-focused hobbyist gardeners seeking location-specific guidance)
- **SOM (Year 1)**: $25K - $75K (freemium model with affiliate revenue + premium PDF sales)
- **SOM (Year 3)**: $250K - $750K (established SEO presence + print-on-demand expansion)

**Market Opportunity Assessment**: **MEDIUM-HIGH**
- Large and growing gardening market with strong online engagement
- Underserved niche in location-specific, accessible gardening resources
- Digital-to-physical model (PDF guides) addresses preference for printable resources
- Accessibility focus (ADHD-friendly) taps into growing neurodiversity awareness
- Multiple revenue streams: affiliate commissions, premium guides, print-on-demand

**Go/No-Go Recommendation**: **GO** - Proceed with MVP focusing on free PDF generation with affiliate links, validate demand for premium/print-on-demand options

**Characteristics**:
- Prefer clear, scannable layouts
- Value printable/offline resources
- Appreciate structured, organized information
- Willing to pay for well-designed accessible tools

**Pain Points**:
- Overwhelming information overload
- Poor information design in existing resources

- Executive function challenges with digital-only tools

## Competitive Landscape

### Direct Competitors

**1. Seed Company Websites (Johnny's, Baker Creek, etc.)**
- **Strengths**: Comprehensive seed catalogs, established brands, direct sales
- **Weaknesses**: Not location-specific, overwhelming catalogs, not accessible-formatted
- **Market Position**: Dominant in seed sales, weak in location-based discovery

**2. Gardening Apps (Gardenize, Planter, etc.)**
- **Strengths**: Digital tools, some location features
- **Weaknesses**: Not printable, complex interfaces, not accessibility-focused
- **Market Position**: Growing but fragmented

**3. Extension Service Websites (USDA, state extensions)**
- **Strengths**: Authoritative, location-specific, free
- **Weaknesses**: Not user-friendly, not scannable, not seed-focused
- **Market Position**: Trusted but difficult to use

### Indirect Competitors

**1. Generic Gardening Websites (Gardening Know How, etc.)**
- **Strengths**: Broad content, SEO presence
- **Weaknesses**: Not location-specific, not seed-focused, not printable
- **Market Position**: High traffic but low specificity

**2. Seed Finder (Our own experiment)**
- **Strengths**: Location-based discovery, native/adapted/invasive tracking
- **Weaknesses**: Not printable, not accessibility-focused
- **Relationship**: Complementary - could integrate or differentiate

**3. Printable Planner Tools (Best Day Ever, etc.)**
- **Strengths**: Accessibility focus, printable format expertise
- **Weaknesses**: Not gardening-specific, not location-based
- **Relationship**: Learn from accessibility design principles

### Competitive Positioning

**Unique Differentiators:**
1. **Location-Based + Printable**: Only product combining zip code-specific seed guides with printable PDF format
2. **Accessibility-Focused**: ADHD-friendly formatting (scannable, clear, organized)
3. **Native/Adapted/Invasive Focus**: Environmental awareness and responsible gardening
4. **Digital-to-Physical**: Bridges online discovery with offline use
5. **SEO-Optimized**: Long-tail search capture ("seeds for zip code X")

**Competitive Advantages:**
- First-mover in location-based printable seed guides

- Multiple revenue streams (affiliate, premium, print-on-demand)
- Low barrier to entry (free guides drive traffic)
- Scalable (automated PDF generation for all zip codes)

**Competitive Risks:**
- Seed companies could add location-based features
- Established gardening sites could add printable guides
- Low barriers to entry for competitors
- Need to maintain SEO advantage
