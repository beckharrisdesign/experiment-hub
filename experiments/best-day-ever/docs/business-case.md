# Best Day Ever — Business Case

## Hypothesis

There is an unmet need for a tool that closes the loop between digital and physical planning — letting people move seamlessly between the two and work wherever they work best.

---

## Why This Matters to Me

I have ADHD and use a digital calendar, and the gap between knowing what's on my schedule and actually starting is something I hit every day.

---

## Who It's For

Adults with ADHD (diagnosed or self-identified) who use a digital calendar, have experienced the benefit of writing things down, and have a printer. Also useful for anyone who prefers working from paper but keeps their schedule digitally — parents, freelancers, students.

---

## What It Does

- Calendar connection: Google Calendar OAuth (read-only). Pull today's events. One-time setup.
- Template selection: 4 layouts — Time Block, Top 3 + Schedule, Week at a Glance, Hybrid Day.
- PDF generation: Print-optimized for US Letter and A4, <5 seconds, black-and-white ready.
- Light personalization: Default template, paper size, day start/end time, calendar selection.

Not in MVP: Apple Calendar / Outlook, custom templates, mobile app, task management, writing to calendar.

Future phase: scan and ingest your completed physical plan back into your calendar and notes — closing the loop from paper back to digital.

## Market

| Segment | Size | Basis |
|---|---|---|
| Total market | $200M–$350M | ADHD + productivity tool addressable segments, bottom-up |
| Reachable segment | $100M–$175M | ADHD/productivity users seeking calendar-integrated printable output |
| Year 1 target | $280K | ~3,500 subscribers at $8/mo |
| Year 3 target | $2.8M | If messaging works and retention holds |

13–16M US adults have ADHD; most already use a digital calendar. Willingness-to-pay signal is clear from adjacent markets — ADHD-specific apps and premium planner brands both have healthy paying bases. Numbers are bottom-up estimates; treat Year 3 as directional.

---

## Existing Options

| Product | Price | Strength | Limitation |
|---|---|---|---|
| Passion Planner / Erin Condren | $30–60/yr | Loyal followings, premium feel | Blank pages — still requires manual calendar transcription |
| Notion / Todoist | Free–$15/mo | Powerful, flexible | Stays on screen; no physical output |
| Google / Apple Calendar | Free | Already in the workflow | Print output is ugly; no ADHD-aware layout |
| Focus Keeper / Forest | Free–$5 | Helps with in-task focus | Addresses focus *during* tasks, not planning *before* them |

No tool takes your real calendar and produces a physical, ADHD-optimized daily plan. That's what's missing.

---

## Biggest Unknown

Whether ADHD adults will recognize this as a distinct problem from "I need a better productivity app" — the messaging has to create the category before the product can fill it.

---
