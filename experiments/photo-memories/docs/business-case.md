# Photo Memories — Business Case

## Hypothesis

Connecting **Google Photos** to thoughtful poster and book formats—then print-on-demand—can win a slice of a large photo-book market, because the hard part is curation and ease, not lack of photos.

---

## Why This Matters to Me

Most photos never leave the phone; the gap between “I have 40,000 images” and “I have something beautiful on the wall” is a product problem worth solving if the UX is genuinely low-friction.

---

## Who It's For

Consumers (especially mobile-first) who use Google Photos heavily, want physical artifacts, and will pay for convenience and good templates—not for another full editor like Canva.

---

## What It Does

- OAuth connection to Google Photos (read-appropriate scope)
- Curated formats: posters, books, and light edits before order
- Print-on-demand fulfillment—no inventory
- **Differentiators to prove:** native library integration, format quality, and low-friction layout vs. incumbents

Not in early MVP: perfect parity with every Shutterfly product line.

---

## Market

| Segment                                                | Size        | Basis                                                    |
| ------------------------------------------------------ | ----------- | -------------------------------------------------------- |
| Total market (global photo books + personalized print) | $2.8B–$4.5B | Industry reports on photo book / POD growth              |
| Reachable segment (US digital-first)                   | $420M–$675M | US consumer segment likely to start from cloud libraries |
| Year 1 target                                          | $50K–$200K  | Early adopter, limited marketing                         |
| Year 3 target                                          | $500K–$2M   | Optimized conversion and repeat purchase                 |

---

## Existing Options

| Product                    | Position                     | Limitation for this bet                             |
| -------------------------- | ---------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------- |
| Shutterfly                 | Market leader, broad catalog | Heavy flow; not Google-native first                 |
| Mixbook, Artifact Uprising | Quality books/posters        | Weaker GPhotos story                                |
| Google Photos print        | Native integration           | Limited customization, not a “memory product” focus |
| Canva print                | Design-first                 | Not photo-library-first; more user effort           |

**Gap:** A **Google-Photos-native**, modern-format, low-friction path to print—if execution and trust in print quality match big brands.

---

## Biggest Unknown

**Trust and quality:** incumbents own brand trust on print. You must match perceived quality and shipping reliability or CAC will eat margin.

---

## Validation Plan

|                | Detail                                                                             |
| -------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Method         | End-to-end MVP: connect, generate, order a narrow set of formats                   |
| Traffic        | Targeted social + organic; measure conversion to paid print                        |
| Budget         | Ads optional; main cost is time + print samples + support                          |
| Success        | Repeatable happy-path orders with low refund/chargeback                            |
| Decision point | If users won’t pay shipping + margin over alternatives, narrow formats or position |

**Status (hub):** Abandoned.

## Scorecard

| Dimension                 | Score     | Criterion met                                          |
| ------------------------- | --------- | ------------------------------------------------------ |
| B — Business opportunity  | 4/5       | Large market; many competitors                         |
| P — Personal impact       | 4/5       | Directly about preserving and enjoying memory          |
| C — Competitive advantage | 3/5       | Differentiation is UX + GPhotos, not a moat on supply  |
| $ — Platform cost         | 3/5       | Integrations, QA, and print ops add load               |
| S — Social impact         | 4/5       | Family memory, joy, accessibility of printed artifacts |
| **Total**                 | **18/25** | **Wins on UX+trust, not TAM**                          |

---

## Recommendation

**GO** only with ruthless scope: a **tiny** set of excellent formats, obsessive quality checks, and clear positioning vs. Shutterfly’s breadth. The market is real; the risk is being another me-too print shop without a sharp wedge.
