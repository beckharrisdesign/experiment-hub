# Seed Finder — Business Case

## Hypothesis

A content-driven site that combines a comprehensive seed database with educational content—and earns through affiliate partnerships—can capture meaningful affiliate revenue in the gardening vertical while helping gardeners discover varietals that actually suit their conditions.

---

## Why This Matters to Me

Gardening content is everywhere, but trustworthy, seed-specific discovery that respects hardiness and sourcing is still fragmented. I want to test whether a focused database plus content can win SEO and trust.

---

## Who It's For

Hobbyist home gardeners who are passionate about seed starting and discovering new varietals, and who already shop seeds and supplies online (affiliate-friendly behavior).

---

## What It Does

- Aggregate seed and varietal information with enough depth to be useful in search and comparison
- Pair educational content with database-driven pages to earn affiliate commissions on seed and supply purchases
- **Prototype note:** a local app validated zip-to-hardiness matching and basic browsing; a full product would be a deployed content site, not the local prototype only

Not in MVP: full production deployment, SEO program, and affiliate relationships were never completed before deprioritizing.

---

## Market

| Segment                       | Size        | Basis                                                                   |
| ----------------------------- | ----------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Total market (affiliate pool) | $3M–$18M    | Bottom-up: niche seed content site affiliate + display revenue at scale |
| Reachable segment             | $2M–$8M     | Realistic revenue ceiling for one strong seed-focused site              |
| Year 1 target                 | $50K–$150K  | Affiliate revenue from seed/supply sales                                |
| Year 3 target                 | $500K–$1.5M | Established SEO + traffic                                               |

Competitive but fragmented; Gardening Know How and seed retailers prove demand for online gardening research, but a seed-deep niche remains differentiated.

---

## Existing Options

| Product                                           | Price           | Strength           | Limitation                                  |
| ------------------------------------------------- | --------------- | ------------------ | ------------------------------------------- |
| Gardening Know How, The Spruce                    | Free (ads)      | Traffic, broad SEO | Seed info is secondary, not a full database |
| Mother Earth News Seed Finder                     | Free            | Many catalogs      | Dated UX, limited depth per seed            |
| Individual seed companies (Johnny’s, Baker Creek) | Catalog pricing | Rich product pages | Siloed; weak cross-vendor discovery         |

**Gap:** No dominant player offers comprehensive seed information plus modern UX plus a clear affiliate-first content model in one place.

---

## Biggest Unknown

Whether SEO and conversion economics work at the scale needed—affiliate CTR, order values, and time-to-rank in a crowded gardening SERP are all uncertain.

---

## Validation Plan

|                | Detail                                                              |
| -------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| Method         | Content-first MVP with measurable affiliate and traffic milestones  |
| Traffic        | Organic SEO + optional paid tests on high-intent seed queries       |
| Budget         | Content + tooling + hosting; scale with early revenue               |
| Success        | Sustained traffic growth and positive unit economics on affiliate   |
| Decision point | If CAC/SEO payback is too long, pivot scope or focus before scaling |

**Status (hub):** Abandoned—local prototype work did not turn into a shipped content business.

## Recommendation

Market research said **GO** on a content + affiliate path. The experiment stopped at a local technical prototype; before revisiting, you’d need a clear decision on whether the next step is **shipping** a small vertical of pages and measuring affiliate reality—not more prototype features alone.
