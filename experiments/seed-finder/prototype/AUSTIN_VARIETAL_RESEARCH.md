# Austin, Texas (Zone 9) - Varietal Recommendations Research

## Overview

This document compiles research on specific varietals recommended for Austin, Texas (Zone 9, hardiness zone 9) for our initial seed list. Focus is on finding specific cultivars/varieties that perform well in Austin's hot, humid climate.

**Location**: Austin, Texas  
**Hardiness Zone**: 9  
**Climate**: Hot summers, mild winters, high humidity  
**Growing Season**: Long (February/March - November/December)

---

## Research Findings

### 1. Roma Tomatoes (Paste Tomatoes)

**General Roma Tomato Characteristics**:
- Determinate or semi-determinate growth habit
- Thick-walled, meaty fruits ideal for sauces and canning
- Typically 2-4 oz fruits, 3-4 inches long

**Heat-Tolerant Roma Varieties for Texas/Austin**:

#### Recommended Varieties:

1. **San Marzano** (Heirloom)
   - Classic Italian paste tomato
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Moderate - may need afternoon shade in extreme heat
   - **Notes**: Not the most heat-tolerant, but classic flavor
   - **Source**: Traditional favorite, widely available

2. **Roma VF** (Disease-resistant hybrid)
   - Improved Roma with disease resistance
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Good - better than standard Roma
   - **Notes**: VF = Verticillium and Fusarium resistant
   - **Source**: Common commercial variety

3. **Amish Paste** (Heirloom)
   - Large paste tomato (4-8 oz)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Good
   - **Notes**: More heat tolerant than San Marzano, excellent flavor
   - **Source**: Popular heirloom choice

4. **Opalka** (Heirloom)
   - Long, pointed paste tomato
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Good
   - **Notes**: Polish heirloom, performs well in heat
   - **Source**: Heirloom seed sources

5. **Juliet** (Hybrid)
   - Small paste tomato (1-2 oz)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Excellent
   - **Notes**: Cherry-sized paste tomato, very heat tolerant
   - **Source**: Hybrid variety

**Austin-Specific Recommendation**: 
- **Primary**: **Roma VF** or **Amish Paste** (best heat tolerance + flavor)
- **Alternative**: **Juliet** (if you want smaller fruits, excellent heat tolerance)

### 2. Zinnias

**General Zinnia Characteristics**:
- Annual flowers, full sun
- Heat and drought tolerant
- Long blooming season (summer through fall)

**Heat-Tolerant Zinnia Varieties for Austin**:

#### Recommended Series/Varieties:

1. **Profusion Series** (Hybrid: Z. elegans × Z. angustifolia)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Excellent
   - **Disease Resistance**: High (powdery mildew resistant)
   - **Characteristics**: Compact (12-18"), continuous blooms, mounding habit
   - **Varieties**: Profusion Cherry, Profusion White, Profusion Orange
   - **Why for Austin**: Specifically bred for heat and humidity tolerance
   - **Source**: Award-winning series, widely recommended for hot climates

2. **Zahara Series** (Hybrid)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Excellent
   - **Disease Resistance**: High (mildew, leaf spot resistant)
   - **Characteristics**: Medium-sized blooms, single/semi-double flowers
   - **Varieties**: Zahara Starlight Rose, Zahara Raspberry Ripple
   - **Why for Austin**: Exceptionally hardy, heat and drought tolerant
   - **Source**: Bred for hot, humid climates

3. **Benary's Giant Series** (Z. elegans)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Good
   - **Characteristics**: Large blooms (6" across), tall (3-4'), long stems
   - **Why for Austin**: Good heat tolerance, excellent for cut flowers
   - **Source**: Popular for large blooms

4. **State Fair Mix** (Z. elegans)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Good
   - **Characteristics**: Large, bold flowers, tall (3-4')
   - **Why for Austin**: Classic variety, good heat tolerance
   - **Source**: Traditional favorite

5. **Zinnia angustifolia** (Narrowleaf Zinnia)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Excellent
   - **Drought Tolerance**: Excellent
   - **Characteristics**: Compact, small daisy-like flowers, spreading habit
   - **Varieties**: Crystal White, Star Gold
   - **Why for Austin**: Native to Southwest, extremely heat/drought tolerant
   - **Source**: Native species, ideal for hot climates

**Austin-Specific Recommendation**:
- **Primary**: **Profusion Series** or **Zahara Series** (best heat + disease resistance)
- **Alternative**: **Zinnia angustifolia** varieties (most heat/drought tolerant, native)

### 3. Basil

**Heat-Tolerant Basil Varieties for Austin**:

1. **Genovese Basil** (Standard)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Good (with adequate water)
   - **Notes**: Classic Italian basil, may bolt in extreme heat
   - **Source**: Most common variety

2. **Thai Basil** (Ocimum basilicum var. thyrsiflora)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Excellent
   - **Notes**: More heat tolerant than Genovese, purple stems
   - **Source**: Asian variety, very heat tolerant

3. **Lemon Basil** (Ocimum × citriodorum)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Good
   - **Notes**: Citrus flavor, good heat tolerance
   - **Source**: Hybrid variety

4. **Spicy Globe Basil** (Compact)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Good
   - **Notes**: Compact growth, good for containers
   - **Source**: Dwarf variety

**Austin-Specific Recommendation**:
- **Primary**: **Thai Basil** (best heat tolerance)
- **Alternative**: **Genovese** (classic, but needs consistent water in heat)

### 4. Lettuce

**Heat-Tolerant Lettuce Varieties for Austin**:

**Note**: Lettuce is a cool-season crop in Austin. For summer, consider heat-tolerant alternatives.

1. **Black Seeded Simpson** (Leaf)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Moderate
   - **Notes**: Fast-growing, good for early spring/fall
   - **Source**: Classic variety

2. **Buttercrunch** (Butterhead)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Moderate
   - **Notes**: Slower to bolt than some varieties
   - **Source**: Popular butterhead

3. **Romaine/Cos** varieties
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Better than leaf lettuce
   - **Notes**: More heat tolerant than loose-leaf types
   - **Source**: Generally more heat tolerant

**Austin-Specific Recommendation**:
- **Primary**: Grow in **early spring (Feb-Apr)** or **fall (Sep-Nov)**
- **Summer Alternative**: Consider heat-tolerant greens (Swiss chard, Malabar spinach)
- **Best Lettuce**: **Romaine varieties** for slightly better heat tolerance

### 5. Marigolds

**Heat-Tolerant Marigold Varieties for Austin**:

1. **French Marigold** (Tagetes patula)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Excellent
   - **Characteristics**: Compact, small flowers, pest-repellent
   - **Source**: Very heat tolerant

2. **African Marigold** (Tagetes erecta)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Excellent
   - **Characteristics**: Large flowers, tall plants
   - **Source**: Excellent heat tolerance

3. **Signet Marigold** (Tagetes tenuifolia)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Excellent
   - **Characteristics**: Small, edible flowers, fine foliage
   - **Source**: Very heat tolerant

**Austin-Specific Recommendation**:
- **All marigold types** perform well in Austin's heat
- **Primary**: **French Marigold** (compact, pest-repellent)
- **Alternative**: **African Marigold** (large blooms)

### 6. Sunflowers

**Heat-Tolerant Sunflower Varieties for Austin**:

1. **Mammoth Sunflower** (Helianthus annuus)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Excellent
   - **Characteristics**: Very tall (10-12'), large heads
   - **Source**: Classic variety, very heat tolerant

2. **Lemon Queen** (Helianthus annuus)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Excellent
   - **Characteristics**: Pale yellow, branching habit
   - **Source**: Heat tolerant, good for pollinators

3. **Autumn Beauty Mix** (Helianthus annuus)
   - **Heat Tolerance**: Excellent
   - **Characteristics**: Multi-colored, branching
   - **Source**: Heat tolerant mix

**Austin-Specific Recommendation**:
- **All sunflower varieties** perform excellently in Austin
- **Primary**: **Mammoth** (classic) or **Autumn Beauty Mix** (colorful)

## Key Considerations for Austin

1. **Heat Tolerance**: Primary concern - summers are very hot (90-100°F+)
2. **Disease Resistance**: High humidity can cause fungal issues
3. **Water Needs**: Consistent watering important in heat
4. **Timing**: Cool-season crops (lettuce) best in spring/fall
5. **Afternoon Shade**: Some plants benefit from afternoon shade in extreme heat
